# SEO penalty recovery record

## Recovery mode

Recovery mode was enabled on July 20, 2026. Ranking recovery is not guaranteed. Automated publishing, indexing submission, growth mutation, and link-building commands are disabled until the penalty evidence and retained content have been reviewed.

`shared/seo-policy.json` is the authoritative indexability policy. A page omitted from that policy must remain out of sitemaps and receive `noindex`.

## Evidence required

Record these items before changing another URL cohort:

- exact Google Search Console manual-action or security-issue text;
- affected scope, example URLs, notification date, and current status;
- current GSC page/query/indexing and crawl-stat exports;
- GA4 organic landing-page and qualified-conversion baseline;
- complete backlink export with source, target, anchor, first-seen date, and status.

Store operational exports in an approved private evidence store, not this public repository.

## URL decisions

Every public URL must receive one documented decision:

- retain and improve;
- consolidate into a stronger page;
- temporary `noindex`;
- permanent 404/410;
- 301 only when a genuinely equivalent destination exists.

The initial retained cohorts preserve pages with recorded clicks or explicit near-page-one evidence. Retention is provisional, not editorial approval. Every retained YMYL page still requires an originality, source, claim, and professional-review assessment.

## Editorial gate

Before adding a URL to the policy, record:

1. intended user need and target query;
2. named editor and update owner;
3. originality and cannibalization review;
4. primary or authoritative sources and verification date;
5. status of professional review where jurisdiction-specific legal information warrants it;
6. unsupported-claim and structured-data review;
7. final disposition and approval date.

Database-authored articles have no review-evidence fields, so they remain `noindex` and outside discoverability inventories.

## Link remediation

PBNs, paid link insertions, expired-domain redirects, satellite networks, disguised community seeding, and duplicate third-party publishing are prohibited. Document suspicious links, request removal first, and use Google's disavow process only when the evidence and Google guidance justify it.

## Reconsideration log

Do not submit a reconsideration request unless Search Console confirms a manual action. When one exists, document the original violation, affected cohorts, removed or corrected material, outreach/removal evidence, remaining limitations, and dates. Submit a factual account without promising rankings or claiming completion that cannot be verified.

## Measurement

Track manual-action status, valid indexed URLs, excluded cohorts, crawl waste, branded and non-branded clicks, qualified organic conversions, and Core Web Vitals. Evaluate by cohort and user outcome rather than URL, schema, or submission volume.
