## The Solar Contract Trap: How It Happens

The residential solar industry exploded between 2015 and 2024. Companies like Sunrun, SunPower, Vivint Solar, Tesla Solar, Freedom Forever, ADT Solar, and Sunnova deployed armies of door-to-door sales representatives across the country, targeting homeowners in high-sun markets like California, Arizona, Texas, Florida, Nevada, and North Carolina.

The sales pitch was almost always the same: *"You'll save $200 a month on your electric bill. The federal tax credit covers 30%. You'll break even in 7 years and own a system that adds value to your home."*

What many homeowners were not told:

- Solar loans often include **escalator clauses** that raise payments 2–3% per year, while utility rates may not rise as fast as projected.
- **Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs)** and solar leases mean you never own the system — you are renting it for 20–25 years.
- The **federal tax credit (ITC)** only applies if you have sufficient federal tax liability. Many homeowners were told they qualified when they did not.
- Net metering policy changes — like California's NEM 3.0 — dramatically reduced the value of solar systems sold under previous assumptions.
- Solar companies frequently **go bankrupt**, leaving homeowners with orphaned systems and no warranty support.

## Your Legal Rights: The Laws That Protect You

Here is what most homeowners do not know: **federal and state consumer protection laws provide powerful tools to cancel solar contracts** when deceptive practices were involved.

### Federal Protections

**Truth in Lending Act (TILA)** — If your solar installation was financed through a loan, TILA requires full disclosure of all financing terms. Violations — including failure to disclose the Annual Percentage Rate, total finance charges, or payment schedule — can give you the right to rescind the contract.

**FTC 3-Day Right of Rescission** — For contracts signed at your home (which most solar contracts are), the FTC's Cooling-Off Rule gives you three business days to cancel without penalty. If the sales representative did not provide you with written notice of this right, the rescission period may still be open — even years later.

**FTC Act Section 5** — Prohibits unfair or deceptive acts in commerce. Misrepresentations about savings, tax credits, or system performance can constitute violations.

### State-Level Protections

Every state has consumer protection statutes that can be applied to deceptive solar sales. Some of the strongest include:

## The 7 Most Common Grounds to Cancel a Solar Contract

Based on thousands of solar contract disputes, these are the most frequently successful legal grounds for cancellation:

**1. Misrepresentation of energy savings.** If the sales representative told you that you would save a specific dollar amount per month and those savings never materialized, that may constitute fraudulent misrepresentation or a violation of your state's consumer protection law.

**2. Inflated production estimates.** Solar companies are required to provide accurate production estimates based on your roof's orientation, shading, and local climate data. If your system consistently underperforms the contractual guarantee, you have grounds for breach of contract.

**3. Failure to disclose the FTC right of rescission.** If you were not given a written notice of your right to cancel within three business days at the time of signing, the rescission window may still be legally open.

**4. TILA violations in your financing documents.** Solar loans are consumer credit transactions subject to TILA. Missing or incorrect disclosures can give you the right to rescind the financing agreement.

**5. Undisclosed escalator clauses.** If your contract contains payment escalators that were not clearly explained during the sales process, this may constitute a material omission under consumer protection law.

**6. Misrepresentation of the federal tax credit.** If you were told you would receive a tax credit that you were not actually eligible for, and this representation was a material factor in your decision to sign, you may have a claim for fraudulent inducement.

**7. Solar company bankruptcy or transfer of contract.** If your solar company has gone bankrupt or sold your contract to a third party without your consent, you may have grounds to challenge the enforceability of the agreement.

## The Process: How Solar Contract Cancellation Works

A typical solar contract cancellation case follows this process:

**Step 1 — Contract Review (Week 1).** A consumer protection attorney reviews your solar contract, financing documents, and any written communications from the sales representative. They identify applicable legal grounds for cancellation.

**Step 2 — Demand Letter (Week 2–3).** The attorney sends a formal demand letter to the solar company asserting the legal grounds for cancellation and demanding release of the contract and any associated lien on your property.

**Step 3 — Negotiation (Weeks 3–8).** Most cases resolve through negotiation. Solar companies — particularly large ones like Sunrun and SunPower — have legal teams that regularly settle these disputes to avoid litigation.

**Step 4 — Resolution (30–90 Days).** The majority of cases are resolved within 30 to 90 days. Resolution typically includes cancellation of the contract, release of any UCC lien on your property, and in some cases, a refund of payments made.

## What to Do Right Now

If you are currently trapped in a solar contract and believe you were misled, here is what to do immediately:

1. **Gather your documents.** Locate your original solar contract, financing agreement, any written proposals or savings estimates you received, and your utility bills from before and after installation.

3. **Do not sign any modification agreements** the solar company sends you. These often contain language that waives your right to pursue cancellation.
